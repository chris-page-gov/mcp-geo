🚶 Walk the Streets of Harold Wood

Starting at Harold Wood Station

Step off the train at Harold Wood station and you're immediately on Station Road — the commercial spine of this suburban Essex neighbourhood. At number 11, there's Missy Moo Balloons, a cheerful shop front, and just up at 35 you'll pass Williams Bakery & Sandwich Bar, the kind of independent high-street trader that gives Harold Wood its village feel. Above the shops, flats at 41A and 43B tell the familiar story of London's intensification — retail below, residential above. 📍 Street View: Station Rd

Along Gubbins Lane

Head north-west from the station and you're on Gubbins Lane, the busiest road in our sample. The NGD data tells us it's a B Road (B175) — classified, with an average width of around 8.5m. Street lighting? Fully lit. Pavement coverage is 100%, with an average width of 1.7m on each side. At number 69, the semi-detached houses begin — classic 1930s London suburban stock. Then at 63, Bell Flower Lodge rises — a modern apartment block with at least 74 flats, its presence signalling the newer development that has reshaped parts of Harold Wood. Up at 120A, terraced housing returns. 📍 Street View: Gubbins Lane

The Residential Heart: Athelstan Road & Ethelburga Road

Turn east off Gubbins Lane and you're into the Anglo-Saxon named streets that define Harold Wood's residential character. Athelstan Road is a quiet local road — around 6.6m wide, fully lit, pavement on both sides. It runs from number 2 at the southern end (semi-detached, RM3 0QB) all the way to the 50s. At number 31, the semi-detacheds are classic interwar builds. The community hub sits here too — Athelstan Hall, classified as a public/community facility, anchoring the neighbourhood. 📍 Street View: Athelstan Hall

Cross over to Ethelburga Road and the pattern continues — terraces at number 33, converted flats at 4B and 6C, and at the eastern end, St. Ethelburga Court, a purpose-built apartment block with at least 16 units. The road is unclassified, around 5.7m wide, and fully lit. 📍 Street View: St Ethelburga Court

The Drive & The Ridgeway — Leafier Ground

Head west and you reach The Drive, where the housing steps up. At number 3, there are terraces, but by 45 and 53 you're among the detached and semi-detached homes of RM3 0DU — wider plots, deeper front gardens. This is the more aspirational end of Harold Wood. Parallel to it, The Ridgeway carries similar character — semi-detacheds at numbers 12, 43, and 54, along a fully lit local road with pavement on both sides. The road data shows 100% pavement coverage and an average width of 7.4m. 📍 Street View: The Drive

The New Harold Wood: Wildcary Lane & St Clements Avenue

South-west of the station, the streets tell a different story. Wildcary Lane, Charlock Close, Safflower Lane, Huntingdon Drive — these are the new-build streets of the Harold Hill/Harold Wood regeneration. The UPRNs here are overwhelmingly flats: Thistle House, Wayfaring Court, Baneberry Lodge. The road data for St Clements Avenue is notable — it shows 100% cycle lane coverage, one of the few roads in our sample with any cycle infrastructure at all. Average road width is 7.8m with good pavement (average 1.98m wide). 📍 Street View: Wildcary Lane area

The Industrial Edge: Church Road & Bates Estate

Follow Church Road north-east and you hit Bates Industrial Estate — the commercial backbone. Here you'll find International Water Solution (Unit 1A, workshop/light industrial), Essex Electric (Unit 1E), and warehouse units at number 26. This is Harold Wood's working edge — away from the residential streets, where the postcode transitions from homes to enterprise. 📍 Street View: Bates Industrial Estate

Southward: Squirrels Heath Road & Hamilton Drive

The southern boundary of our sample area runs along Squirrels Heath Road — a mix of semi-detached and detached homes. Number 149 is a detached property, 152 and 154 are semis. Turn into Hamilton Drive and you find a long residential street with terraces at 99 and 101, and converted flats at 94 and 121. The road link data shows it's fully lit with 100% pavement coverage and an average road width of about 7m. 📍 Street View: Squirrels Heath Rd
